1. Key Elements to Prepare the Guru
Materials Overview:
Provide the Guru with the following essential materials:
1.	Energy Sprint Overview: The primary document outlining the structure and objectives.
2.	Energy Deep Dive: Detailed analysis covering sleep, food, movement, and mental energy.
3.	Fast Track Protocols:
o	Sleep Protocol
o	Nutrition & Eat Protocol
o	Movement Protocol
o	Brain Use Protocol
4.	Brain Juice Prompts: Questions and challenges to guide deeper reflection.
5.	Team Preparation and Individual Reflection Templates: Guidelines for the team to complete pre-sprint.
Key Concepts:
1.	Energy as a Foundation: Emphasize the importance of energy management, not time management.
2.	Physical and Mental Energy: Explain the role of sleep, nutrition, movement, and brain function in energy optimization.
3.	Leadership Starts with Energy: Reinforce how leaders must manage their energy to lead their teams effectively.
Discussion Points:
1.	Maximizing Energy: How better sleep, nutrition, movement, and brain habits will directly improve leadership.
2.	Team Accountability: Focus on how the team can support each other in maintaining high energy.
3.	Performance Impact: Discuss the measurable outcomes of better energy management.

2. Key Elements to Prepare the Team
Team Preparation Guidelines:
•	Pre-Sprint Reflection: Ensure all team members have completed their energy tracking sheets, reflecting on sleep, food, movement, and mental routines.
•	Materials Distribution: Ensure all team members have received the Brain Juice and Deep Dive documents in advance.
•	Role Clarity: Each member should be aware of their role in the team discussion and reflection process.
Key Discussion Points:
1.	Personal Energy Insights: Every team member shares key energy-draining habits and one immediate action to improve.
2.	Team Energy Rituals: Brainstorm ways to support each other as a team (e.g., team exercise breaks, nutrition tips, sleep quality discussions).

3. Analysis of Initial Personality Assessment
Team Dynamics Overview:
•	Supportive Team Members: Identify high-energy leaders who naturally maintain strong energy routines and can motivate others.
•	Potential Challenges: Note any team members who may struggle with managing their energy or could disrupt the focus (e.g., lack of sleep, overworking).
Mitigation Strategies:
•	For Low-Energy Individuals: Suggest focusing on one key area (e.g., improving sleep first) to build momentum.
•	For High-Energy Leaders: Leverage their habits as examples for the team to emulate.
________________________________________
4. Possible Difficulties and Questions from the Team
Anticipated Difficulties:
1.	Time Management vs. Energy Management: Some may focus too much on managing time rather than energy.
2.	Overwhelmed by Change: Team members may feel overwhelmed by trying to change too many energy habits at once.
3.	Skepticism About Impact: Some may doubt how much these changes will actually improve their performance.
Preparation Strategies:
1.	Focus on Quick Wins: Encourage small, actionable steps, like better sleep or short daily movement breaks.
2.	Tie Energy to Performance: Link the benefits of energy optimization directly to increased leadership effectiveness and results.
Common Questions:
1.	How can I manage my energy when my schedule is packed?
o	Answer: Focus on improving sleep quality and adding small movement breaks throughout the day.
2.	What’s the quickest way to boost my energy?
o	Answer: Improve hydration and avoid heavy meals during work hours. Quality sleep is the best long-term solution.
3.	How does this affect our team’s overall productivity?
o	Answer: When the entire team is operating at higher energy levels, decision-making, creativity, and collaboration improve significantly.
