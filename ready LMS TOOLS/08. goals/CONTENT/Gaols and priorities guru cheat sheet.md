# Gaols and priorities guru cheat sheet

**Guru Cheat Sheet for Goals and Priorities Sprint**

**Objective:** Equip the Guru with essential tools and information to effectively facilitate the "Goals and Priorities" sprint meeting, ensuring successful management of discussions, addressing challenges, and steering the team towards the sprint's strategic objectives.

**Document Specifications and Structure:**

**Title Page:**

- **Icon:** A compass (symbolizing strategic navigation and focus)

**Introduction:**

- **Purpose of the Document:** To provide the Guru with a roadmap and toolkit for leading the sprint, emphasizing clarity in goals and efficiency in prioritization as pivotal to driving company performance.
- **Overview of the Sprint:** This session is designed to refine the team's approach to setting impactful goals and identifying high-priority actions that align with our overarching business strategy.

**Detailed Meeting Agenda and Instructions:**

1.  **Goal Review and Setting (30 minutes):**
    - **Instructions:** Start with a discussion on the company’s strategic objectives. Facilitate a collaborative session to define five measurable targets for the next quarter, ensuring these targets are ambitious yet attainable.
    - **Potential Issues:** Vague or overly ambitious goal suggestions.
    - **Handling Strategy:** Encourage precise language that quantifies targets. If goals are too broad, ask probing questions to refine them, such as, "What specific outcome are we aiming to achieve?"
2.  **Idea Brainstorming (15 minutes):**
    - **Instructions:** Lead a structured brainstorming session inviting all team members to propose actions that could help achieve the defined goals. Promote an environment where every suggestion is valued.
    - **Potential Issues:** Dominance of discussion by certain individuals.
    - **Handling Strategy:** Ensure equitable participation by inviting quieter members to share their ideas and by potentially using a round-robin format.
3.  **Prioritization of Ideas (15 minutes):**
    - **Instructions:** Introduce the Impact/Ease matrix and guide the team in evaluating each idea based on its potential impact on goals and the ease of implementation.
    - **Potential Issues:** Confusion on scoring; disagreements on impact versus ease.
    - **Handling Strategy:** Provide clear examples of what constitutes 'high impact' and 'ease'. If disagreements occur, facilitate a vote or make an executive decision to maintain momentum.
4.  **KPI and Accountability Assignment (15 minutes):**
    - **Instructions:** For each top priority, assign quantifiable Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) and designate an executive responsible for each KPI.
    - **Potential Issues:** Hesitation to accept accountability.
    - **Handling Strategy:** Discuss the support structures in place for achieving these priorities and the strategic importance of each role.
5.  **Team Alignment (30 minutes):**
    - **Instructions:** Each executive reviews their department-specific priorities to ensure alignment with the overall company goals.
    - **Potential Issues:** Misalignment or redundancy in departmental priorities.
    - **Handling Strategy:** Adjust priorities on-the-fly to ensure coherence and direct linkage to company goals, facilitating cross-departmental collaboration if necessary.

**Conclusion and Follow-up:**

- **Recap:** Summarize the session’s key decisions and assigned action items.
- **Next Steps:** Schedule follow-up meetings to review progress on priorities and refine strategies as needed.
- **Feedback:** Encourage feedback on the effectiveness of the meeting to continuously improve the sprint process.

**Additional Notes:**

- **Engagement Techniques:** Use polling or breakout sessions to keep the meeting interactive.
- **Documentation:** Assign a scribe to record discussions and decisions for transparency and follow-up.
- **Time Management Tips:** Keep a timer to ensure each agenda item stays on track.

**Final Instructions:** Review this cheat sheet in detail before the meeting, and customize it based on preliminary feedback from the team. Ensure all preparation is complete, including a rehearsal if necessary, to fully embrace your role as the Guru in this strategic sprint.
