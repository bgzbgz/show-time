# Goals and priorities deep dive

**ast Track Practical Tips and Rankings for Deep Dive Resources on Goal Setting, Prioritization, and Planning**

**Section 1: Goal Setting**

**Books**

**1\. "Measure What Matters" by John Doerr**

- **Fast Track Ranking**: 9/10
- **Top 3 Practical Tips**:
    1.  Implement OKRs (Objectives and Key Results) at all organizational levels to align efforts and track progress.
    2.  Use quarterly OKR reviews to adapt and refine goals based on current data and feedback.
    3.  Encourage all team members to set personal OKRs to foster engagement and alignment with company objectives.

**2\. "Atomic Habits" by James Clear**

- **Fast Track Ranking**: 8/10
- **Top 3 Practical Tips**:
    1.  Build new habits by attaching them to existing routines with the strategy of habit stacking.
    2.  Focus on small improvements; achieving 1% better each day leads to significant growth over time.
    3.  Design your environment to reduce friction towards good habits and increase friction for bad habits.

**3\. "The 4 Disciplines of Execution" by Chris McChesney, Sean Covey, and Jim Huling**

- **Fast Track Ranking**: 8.5/10
- **Top 3 Practical Tips**:
    1.  Identify and focus on Wildly Important Goals (WIGs) that will make the most impact.
    2.  Track lead measures that are predictive of reaching your WIGs and that you can influence.
    3.  Create a compelling scoreboard and regularly update it to reflect progress towards goals.

**Section 2: Prioritization**

**Books**

**1\. "Essentialism: The Disciplined Pursuit of Less" by Greg McKeown**

- **Fast Track Ranking**: 9/10
- **Top 3 Practical Tips**:
    1.  Apply the "Less but Better" approach to focus on only the most essential tasks that significantly impact your goals.
    2.  Regularly evaluate all ongoing projects and commitments to ensure they align with your core objectives—be willing to say no to everything else.
    3.  Schedule time blocks for deep work sessions where you can focus on high-priority tasks without interruptions.

**2\. "The One Thing" by Gary Keller with Jay Papasan**

- **Fast Track Ranking**: 8.5/10
- **Top 3 Practical Tips**:
    1.  Identify the one task that will make the most significant impact towards achieving your goals and prioritize it above all else each day.
    2.  Use the focusing question: "What's the ONE Thing I can do such that by doing it everything else will be easier or unnecessary?"
    3.  Cultivate a strong discipline of time management to protect time designated for your ONE thing against other demands.

**3\. "First Things First" by Stephen R. Covey**

- **Fast Track Ranking**: 8/10
- **Top 3 Practical Tips**:
    1.  Prioritize tasks based on a matrix of urgency and importance to focus on what truly drives outcomes rather than just pressing matters.
    2.  Allocate time each week for planning to sort tasks into quadrants of the time management matrix and adjust your schedule accordingly.
    3.  Reflect on your roles and goals weekly to ensure your priorities align with your long-term objectives.

**Articles**

**1\. "How to Prioritize Work and Meet Deadlines When Everything Is #1" (Lifehacker)**

- **Fast Track Ranking**: 7/10
- **Top 3 Practical Tips**:
    1.  Break your workload into smaller, manageable units and prioritize based on deadline and impact.
    2.  Use tools like Trello or Asana to visually organize and prioritize tasks by urgency and importance.
    3.  Establish clear communication channels to manage expectations and renegotiate deadlines if necessary.

**2\. "Art of Prioritization" (Forbes)**

- **Fast Track Ranking**: 7.5/10
- **Top 3 Practical Tips**:
    1.  Regularly reassess priorities based on shifting business needs and personal growth objectives.
    2.  Implement a system for quick decision-making to determine the priority level of new tasks as they arise.
    3.  Train your team on prioritization techniques to ensure alignment and efficient workflow.

**3\. "Mastering the Art of Prioritization" (Harvard Business Review)**

- **Fast Track Ranking**: 8/10
- **Top 3 Practical Tips**:
    1.  Focus on results, not just the tasks; prioritize actions that have a direct impact on your business goals.
    2.  Learn to delegate effectively to free up time for tasks that require your unique expertise.
    3.  Keep a dynamic to-do list that reflects real-time priorities and allows for flexibility as situations change.

**3\. "Prioritizing Tasks - The Key to Time Management" (Lynda.com)**

- **Fast Track Ranking**: 7.5/10
- **Top 3 Practical Tips**:
    1.  Prioritize tasks by potential payoff, focusing first on those that bring the greatest benefits.
    2.  Set realistic deadlines for each task based on their priority to ensure timely completion.
    3.  Utilize time management apps to help maintain focus on high-priority tasks and avoid distractions.

**Section 3: Decision Making**

**Fast Track Top 3 Books on Decision Making**

**1\. "Thinking, Fast and Slow" by Daniel Kahneman**

- **Fast Track Ranking**: 9/10
- **Top 3 Practical Tips**:
    1.  Understand the difference between fast, intuitive thinking (System 1) and slow, rational thinking (System 2) to use them appropriately in decision-making scenarios.
    2.  Be aware of cognitive biases like confirmation bias and anchoring, which can distort rational decision-making.
    3.  Regularly practice critical thinking exercises to improve your System 2 thinking, enhancing your ability to make complex decisions.

**2\. "Decisive: How to Make Better Choices in Life and Work" by Chip Heath and Dan Heath**

- **Fast Track Ranking**: 8.5/10
- **Top 3 Practical Tips**:
    1.  Use the WRAP process: Widen your options, Reality-test your assumptions, Attain distance before deciding, and Prepare to be wrong.
    2.  Challenge the status quo by considering more than just the default options to ensure all possibilities are explored.
    3.  Set up tripwires—defined points at which you'll stop and reconsider your decision—to avoid going too far down the wrong path.

**3\. "Predictably Irrational: The Hidden Forces That Shape Our Decisions" by Dan Ariely**

- **Fast Track Ranking**: 8/10
- **Top 3 Practical Tips**:
    1.  Recognize that our decisions are often influenced by environmental and emotional factors that may not align with our rational interests.
    2.  Conduct controlled experiments, such as A/B testing, to make decisions based on actual data rather than assumptions.
    3.  Regularly review decisions in a different state of mind or environment to spot errors in judgment that were not apparent initially.

**Fast Track Top 3 Articles on Decision Making**

**1\. "How Good Leaders Make Tough Decisions" (Harvard Business Review)**

- **Fast Track Ranking**: 8/10
- **Top 3 Practical Tips**:
    1.  Focus on aligning decisions with core values and long-term strategic objectives to ensure consistency and integrity in leadership.
    2.  Engage diverse viewpoints to challenge your thinking and broaden the range of solutions considered.
    3.  Prioritize transparency in the decision-making process to build trust and accountability.

**2\. "Decision Making in the Workplace: A Unified Perspective" (Forbes)**

- **Fast Track Ranking**: 7.5/10
- **Top 3 Practical Tips**:
    1.  Use decision matrices to quantify and compare the benefits and costs of different options objectively.
    2.  Foster an inclusive decision-making environment where all stakeholders can contribute their perspectives.
    3.  Implement a feedback loop to learn from past decisions and continuously improve decision-making skills.

**3\. "The Psychology of Decision Making" (Psychology Today)**

- **Fast Track Ranking**: 7/10
- **Top 3 Practical Tips**:
    1.  Be conscious of emotional influences on your decisions and strive to separate feelings from facts when analyzing options.
    2.  Avoid decision fatigue by making important decisions at the start of the day when your mental energy is highest.
    3.  Cultivate patience and allow sufficient time for reflection when faced with significant decisions.

**Fast Track Top 3 Videos on Decision Making**

**1\. "Smart Choices: A Practical Guide to Making Better Decisions" (YouTube: Talks at Google)**

- **Fast Track Ranking**: 8.5/10
- **Top 3 Practical Tips**:
    1.  Identify your decision problem clearly and state it precisely to avoid confusion and ensure that you are addressing the right issue.
    2.  Systematically evaluate the pros and cons of each option using a decision-making framework like the decision tree.
    3.  Reassess your decision-making process periodically to refine and adjust based on outcomes and new information.

**2\. "How to Make Rational Decisions in the Face of Uncertainty" (TEDx Talks)**

- **Fast Track Ranking**: 9/10
- **Top 3 Practical Tips**:
    1.  Embrace uncertainty as a natural part of decision-making and use probabilistic thinking to make the best possible choices under unknown conditions.
    2.  Distinguish between risk (where probabilities are known) and uncertainty (where probabilities are not known) to apply appropriate decision-making strategies.
    3.  Use scenario planning to explore different outcomes and prepare strategies for dealing with various future conditions.

**3\. "Critical Thinking and Problem Solving: Effective Decision-Making" (Lynda.com)**

- **Fast Track Ranking**: 8/10
- **Top 3 Practical Tips**:
    1.  Apply critical thinking skills to break down complex problems into manageable parts, improving clarity and decision-making accuracy.
    2.  Challenge assumptions and test hypotheses through experimentation and critical questioning.
    3.  Encourage collaborative problem-solving to leverage collective expertise and gain broader insights into the challenges at hand.
