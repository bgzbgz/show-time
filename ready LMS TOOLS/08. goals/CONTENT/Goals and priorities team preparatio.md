### .**Team Preparation Sheet: Sprint on Setting Goals and Priorities**

**Purpose of the Sprint:** This session is designed to refine our skills in setting strategic goals and actionable priorities using the Fast Track method, ensuring all team members are equipped and aligned to effectively contribute to our company’s objectives.

**Preparation Steps:**

1.  **Review Key Materials:** Familiarize yourself with the provided sprint materials focused on goal setting and prioritization techniques.
2.  **Complete Pre-Assignments:** Finish any assigned tasks or tools for a comprehensive discussion, understanding their strategic impacts.
3.  **Formulate Discussion Points:** Reflect on your current strategies in goal setting and prioritization, prepared to discuss improvements and challenges.

**Expected Meeting Agenda:**

- **Goal Review and Setting (30 minutes):** Establish five specific, measurable company targets for the next quarter.
- **Idea Brainstorming (15 minutes):** Generate a comprehensive list of projects and actions that can directly contribute to achieving these targets.
- **Prioritization of Ideas (15 minutes):**
    - Evaluate ideas using the Impact/Easy matrix (Impact scale 1-3, 3 = most impactful; Ease scale 1-3, 3 = easiest).
    - Calculate scores in Excel with Impact given a weight of 2 and Ease a weight of 1. Identify the top 5 priorities.
- **KPI and Accountability Assignment (15 minutes):** Assign one clear KPI and one executive to each of the top 5 priorities.
- **Team Alignment (30 minutes):** Each executive presents their specific team priorities, checking for alignment with overall company goals.

**What to Bring:**

- **Completed Pre-Assignments**
- **Ideas and KPI Suggestions**

**FAQs:**

1.  **What is the difference between a goal and a priority?**
    - **Goal:** A specific outcome or result to be achieved, e.g., "Increase sales by 20% by the end of Q3."
    - **Priority:** An action to be performed to reach a goal, e.g., "Implement a new sales training program by June 1st."
2.  **How should a priority be formulated to ensure it is actionable?**
    - **Correct Example:** "Deploy targeted marketing campaigns in three key regions by March 15th."
    - **Incorrect Example:** "Focus on marketing efforts."
3.  **What does it mean for a priority to be time-bound?**
    - **Correct Example:** "Complete the upgrade of customer service software by April 20th to improve response time by 30%."
    - **Incorrect Example:** "Work on improving customer service."
4.  **How can we ensure that a priority is directly linked to achieving a specific goal?**
    - **Correct Example:** "Secure partnerships with five major industry players by May 1st to expand market reach."
    - **Incorrect Example:** "Build industry relationships."
5.  **What makes a priority specific and clear?**
    - **Correct Example:** "Reduce manufacturing costs by 15% by renegotiating supplier contracts by the end of Q2."
    - **Incorrect Example:** "Cut manufacturing costs."

**Brainstorming Process:**

- **Objective:** Generate a diverse set of ideas that can potentially achieve the set goals.
- **Method:** Engage in a 15-minute free-form brainstorming session where all team members contribute ideas without criticism.
- **Outcome:** Compile a list of all proposed actions, followed by the application of the Impact/Easy matrix to identify and prioritize the most effective and feasible actions.

**Final Tips:**

- Ensure all tasks are completed prior to the meeting and come prepared with actionable ideas.
- Stay engaged and focused, actively participating in discussions and decision-making.
- Keep all contributions directly aligned with the strategic objectives discussed.
