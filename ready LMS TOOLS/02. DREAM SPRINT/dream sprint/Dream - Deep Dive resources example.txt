Deep Dive: Crafting the Company Dream
Sprint Name: Crafting the Company Dream

1. HBR Building Company Vision
Expected Reading Time: 15 minutes
Reference to Source and Author:
Harvard Business Review Article - "Building Company Vision"

Fast Track 80-20: Key Learnings and Application
1. Core Ideology: The article emphasizes the importance of identifying your company's core ideology-what you stand for. This is foundational in crafting a Dream that endures through time and guides strategic decisions.
2. Envisioned Future: A compelling Dream should paint a vivid picture of the future. It should be ambitious, yet achievable, and inspire everyone in the organization to work toward it.
3. BHAG (Big Hairy Audacious Goal): The concept of a BHAG encourages setting a goal that is so bold it feels almost out of reach. This aligns with the Fast Track principle of pushing beyond comfort zones to achieve extraordinary outcomes.
4. Clarity and Focus: The vision should be clear and focused. Avoid trying to be everything to everyone-know what you stand for and where you're going.
5. Storytelling: Crafting your Dream should involve storytelling that makes it relatable and memorable. People remember stories better than abstract concepts.
6. Living the Dream: It's not enough to articulate a Dream; the leadership must embody and live it every day. This creates a culture that aligns with the vision.
7. Communication: Regularly communicate the Dream throughout the organization. Consistent messaging ensures that everyone remains aligned and motivated, helping to keep the Dream alive in the daily activities of the company.

2. Start with the End in Mind
Expected Reading Time: 10 minutes
Reference to Source and Author:
Stephen R. Covey - "The 7 Habits of Highly Effective People"

Fast Track 80-20: Key Learnings and Application
1. Begin with a Clear Vision: Covey's principle of "Begin with the End in Mind" emphasizes the necessity of having a clear, well-defined vision of what you want to achieve. This directly informs the crafting of a company's Dream, ensuring that every action taken is aligned with this end goal.
2. Mental Creation Precedes Physical Creation: The Dream is first created in the mind before it can be realized in reality. This concept underlines the importance of taking the time to clearly define your Dream before taking action.
3. Alignment with Core Values: The Dream should be in harmony with the core values of both the individuals and the organization. This alignment ensures that the path toward the Dream is sustainable and motivating.
4. Role of Leadership: Leaders must embody the Dream and lead by example. Covey emphasizes that personal victories precede public victories, meaning leaders must first be clear about their own goals and values before guiding the organization.
5. Effective Prioritization: The Dream helps in prioritizing actions and decisions, ensuring that the most important goals are always at the forefront. This aligns with Covey's principle of putting first things first.
6. Long-Term Focus: The Dream should provide a long-term focus, helping the organization stay on course even in the face of short-term challenges.
7. Inspirational Power: The Dream serves as a source of inspiration, motivating individuals to work towards a shared vision and making the journey as important as the destination.

3. Start with Why
Expected Reading Time: 10 minutes
Reference to Source and Author:
Simon Sinek - "Start with Why"

Fast Track 80-20: Key Learnings and Application
1. The Power of Why: Sinek's philosophy centers around the idea that knowing "Why" your company exists is the foundation of success. The Dream must encapsulate this "Why" and communicate it clearly to inspire and align everyone in the organization.
2. Emotional Connection: A Dream should create an emotional connection with both employees and customers. It's not just about what the company does, but why it does it, and how it impacts the world.
3. Leadership and Inspiration: Great leaders inspire action by communicating a compelling Dream. This Dream should not be about achieving financial metrics alone but about making a meaningful impact.
4. Consistency in Action: The Dream should be consistently reflected in every action the company takes. This consistency builds trust and strengthens the alignment between the company's actions and its values.
5. Long-Term Vision: The Dream should be a long-term vision that guides the company through change and challenges, keeping the focus on the bigger picture.
6. Clarity and Simplicity: The Dream should be clear and simple, easily understood by everyone in the organization. This simplicity ensures that the Dream is not just words on a page but a living, breathing part of the company culture.
7. Purpose-Driven Growth: The Dream should drive growth by aligning the company's purpose with its strategic goals, creating a powerful synergy that propels the organization forward.

Each of these deep dives is designed to help you fully understand the concept of crafting a company Dream and how it can be applied effectively within your organization. Reflect on these insights and how they can be integrated into your company's strategic planning and daily operations.
