DREAM Guru Guide: Crafting the Company Dream

Role of the Guru:
As the Guru, your role is to lead and facilitate the team through the process of crafting a bold, vivid, and inspiring Dream for the company. You will guide the discussions, ensure alignment, and help the team overcome any challenges that arise during the sprint. This guide will provide you with all the necessary information to prepare effectively and lead the team meeting successfully.

1. Key Topics of the Sprint
* The Importance of a Company Dream: Understanding why a compelling Dream is essential for the organization's success.
* Crafting the Dream: The process of creating a vivid, bold, and emotionally resonant Dream that aligns with the company's core values.
* Dream Visualization: Bringing the Dream to life through imagery, key words, and associations with influential figures.
* Corporate Killer Exercise: Identifying and articulating the unique value the company brings to the world and ensuring this is reflected in the Dream.

3. Structuring the Discussion and Agenda
A. Introduction (5 minutes):
* Briefly explain the purpose of the sprint and why crafting a Dream is crucial for the company's success.
* Set the tone for the meeting by sharing a powerful quote or story related to the Dream.
B. Presentation of Concepts (10 minutes):
* Present the key concepts from the Brain Juice and the Dream tools.
* Highlight the importance of simplicity, emotional resonance, and alignment with core values.
C. Dream Reflection Discussion (20 minutes):
* Facilitate a discussion where each team member shares their individual reflections from the Company Dream Tool.
* Encourage open, honest sharing and ensure that all voices are heard.
D. Dream Visualization (20 minutes):
* Lead the team in selecting key words, images, and figures for the Dream Board.
* Ensure that the chosen elements align with the company's core values and vision for the future.
E. Finalizing the Dream (20 minutes):
* Synthesize the input from the discussions into a cohesive, one-page Dream narrative.
* Ensure the Dream is simple, bold, and emotionally resonant.
F. Reflection and Next Steps (10 minutes):
* Facilitate a reflection on the decisions made during the meeting.
* Discuss how the Dream will be communicated and integrated into the company's culture.
* Assign tasks for creating the Dream Board and other follow-up actions.

4. Leading the Discussion
* Be Inclusive: Ensure that everyone has an opportunity to contribute. Encourage quieter team members to share their thoughts.
* Keep the Focus: Stay on track by reminding the team of the purpose of the sprint and the importance of the Dream.
* Facilitate, Don't Dictate: Your role is to guide the discussion, not to dominate it. Encourage collaboration and synthesis of ideas.
* Use Visuals: Incorporate the Dream Visualization Tool to help the team see and feel the Dream as it takes shape.

5. Possible Difficulties and How to Avoid Them
o Vagueness in the Dream - Encourage specificity in the discussion. Ask probing questions to get to the core of what the Dream should represent.
o Overcomplicating the Dream - Remind the team of the importance of simplicity. Keep the Dream focused and clear.
o Lack of Emotional Connection -  Guide the team to include elements that resonate emotionally. Use the Corporate Killer Exercise to identify what truly matters.
o Team Misalignment - Facilitate open dialogue to ensure all perspectives are considered. Aim for consensus but be prepared to make a decision if needed.
o Difficulty in Visualization - Use the Dream Visualization Tool to help the team see the Dream. Encourage the use of imagery and metaphors.

6. FAQ for the Sprint
1. Why is crafting a Dream important?
o A Dream provides direction and purpose, aligning everyone in the organization toward a common goal.
2. What if our Dream changes over time?
o The Dream can evolve as the company grows. Regular reviews will ensure it remains relevant and inspiring.
3. How do we ensure the Dream is more than just words?
o Integrate the Dream into daily operations, making it a living, breathing part of the company culture.
4. What if there is disagreement within the team about the Dream?
o Facilitate open discussions to find common ground, ensuring that the final Dream reflects the collective aspirations of the team.
5. Can the Dream be too simple?
o Simplicity is key. A clear, simple Dream is more likely to be understood, embraced, and acted upon by everyone in the organization.
6. How do we make the Dream tangible?
o Use the Dream Visualization Tool to create a visual representation that brings the Dream to life.
7. What if some team members are not fully engaged?
o Encourage participation by highlighting the importance of the Dream for the company's success and individual roles.
8. How often should we revisit the Dream?
o Regularly, especially during significant changes or strategic planning sessions, to ensure it stays relevant.
9. What if our Dream feels too ambitious?
o A Dream should be bold and push boundaries. If it feels too ambitious, break it down into achievable milestones.
10. How do we communicate the Dream effectively?
o Develop a communication plan that includes regular updates, integration into meetings, and visible reminders (e.g., Dream Board).
11. What role does leadership play in living the Dream?
o Leaders must embody the Dream and lead by example. Their actions should consistently reflect the Dream's values.
12. How do we ensure the Dream is inclusive?
o Involve diverse perspectives in its creation and ensure it resonates with everyone in the organization.
